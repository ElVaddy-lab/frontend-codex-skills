# Visual Fidelity Checklist

Compare at matching viewport dimensions whenever possible.

## Structure

- Section order and information architecture match.
- First viewport proves the same primary message or task.
- Primary CTA, product surface, or focal object has the intended prominence.
- Content density and whitespace rhythm are comparable.

## Geometry

- Container width, columns, alignment, and gutters.
- Section heights, padding, gaps, and vertical rhythm.
- Element sizing, aspect ratios, and image crops.
- Sticky, fixed, overlapping, and layered relationships.
- Border radius, borders, shadows, and surface hierarchy.

## Typography

- Font family or approved fallback.
- Weight, size, line height, line length, and wrapping.
- Heading hierarchy and text alignment.
- Button, label, metadata, and body-text proportions.

## Color and Media

- Palette, contrast, backgrounds, accents, and states.
- Image subject, crop, quality, treatment, and placement.
- Icons, illustrations, video, texture, or canvas content.
- No broken assets or unintended placeholders.

## Components and States

- Navigation, buttons, cards, forms, tables, tabs, filters, and modals.
- Hover, focus, active, selected, disabled, loading, empty, error, and success states.
- Motion timing and hierarchy when motion is part of the approved design.

## Precision Rules

- Use measurements when DOM geometry or design dimensions are available.
- Use relative language when the source is an unscaled screenshot.
- Distinguish material mismatch from harmless rendering variance.
- Font rasterization, antialiasing, and minor browser differences are not acceptance failures by themselves.
